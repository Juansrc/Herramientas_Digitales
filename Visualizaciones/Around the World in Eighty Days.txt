Around the World in Eighty Days by Jules Verne is a classic adventure novel published in 1873. The story follows the protagonist, Phileas Fogg, an English gentleman of precise habits, who makes a wager with his fellow members at the Reform Club in London that he can circumnavigate the globe in just eighty days. Accompanied by his loyal servant, Passepartout, Fogg embarks on a journey filled with obstacles, including a detective named Fix who mistakenly believes Fogg to be a bank robber. Along the way, they traverse various countries and encounter a myriad of challenges, from navigating through storms at sea to rescuing a young Indian widow named Aouda. Despite facing numerous setbacks, Fogg remains determined to win the bet and prove that it is indeed possible to travel around the world in such a short amount of time. In the end, Fogg and Passepartout barely make it back to London in time, winning the wager and surprising everyone with their remarkable journey. The novel explores themes of adventure, perseverance, and the transformative power of travel.
